Project - 7 :
Integrated Global Radiosonde Archive ( IGRA )
[2700 Data Stations]
[A] Introduction :

[1] The Integrated Global Radiosonde Archive (IGRA) consists of radiosonde and pilot balloon observations at
over 2,700 globally distributed stations.

[2] The earliest data date back to 1905, and recent data become available in near real time.

[3] Observations are available at standard and variable pressure levels, fixed- and variable-height wind levels,
and the surface and tropopause.

[4] Variables include pressure, temperature, geopotential height, relative humidity, dew point depression,
wind direction and speed, and elapsed time since launch.

[B] Components :

(1) IGRA consists of three components:
[1.1] Individual soundings, organized into one file per station
[1.2] Monthly means, organized into one file per variable and time of day (0000 and 1200 Universal Coordinated Time)
[1.3] Sounding-derived parameters, organized into one file per station

(2) In addition, IGRA station metadata are available to assist in the interpretation of the data. They include current station names and locations as well as information on changes in station location, instrumentation, and observing practices over time, to the extent that they are available.
